New Village began as an idea for the kind of café its neighbourhood didn't have yet — somewhere unhurried, a little green, and built around food that doesn't shout for attention.

Inside, you'll find dim warm lighting, plants tucked into every corner, and seating spread across a calm indoor room and a quieter outdoor patio. It's the kind of place where a single cup of coffee can stretch into an afternoon — favoured by couples on slow dates, friends catching up softly, and regulars who've claimed their corner table.

The kitchen leans toward lighter, European-style flavours — grilled sandwiches, loaded bowls, fresh salads and slow breakfasts — alongside hand-brewed coffee, cold pressed juices and herbal coolers. If you're after something bold and fiery, this isn't that kind of menu; if you're after something calm and considered, you're in the right place.
